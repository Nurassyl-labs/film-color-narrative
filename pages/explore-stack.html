The Geological Layers of Time-Dissected Color

To closely examine the composition differences between movie genres, we dissect every color block in the barcodes, stack them together, and arrange them by brightness, saturation, or hue. This builds a "geological layer" representation of visual identity. For example, Animation color bands are visibly brighter and more saturated, while Biographies lean heavily toward warm, muted colors.
