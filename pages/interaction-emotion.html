We further aggregate narrative segment data across all sample films to examine statistical relationships between color and dialogue. For instance, brighter scenes sometimes display more negative sentiment, which could indicate the use of bright settings to contrast and underscore negative emotions.

Through this section (C), we uncover the deeper interplay between color choices and spoken text. Color systems subtly echo narrative twists, externalizing hidden character arcs and psychological states into immediate visual experiences. Compared to explicit narrative devices like dialogues or camera movement, color operates implicitly, shaping viewer mood, establishing atmosphere, and foreshadowing narrative shifts.
